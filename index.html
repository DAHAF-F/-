Our Story

A courtyard built around one tree, one fire.

The name carries an old Greek saying — «Στην αυλή της μιας μηλιάς», "in the courtyard of the one apple tree" — used to describe a gathering so close-knit it could fit beneath a single tree. That is the room we set out to build: one fire, one table, everyone close enough to share it.

Chef Dimitris Antonakos trained in the charcoal kitchens of Thessaloniki before spending a decade between Lyon and Buenos Aires, learning how distance and live fire change the character of meat. He brought that knowledge home, married it to Aegean ingredients and aged Pakistani beef, and built a menu where the grill does most of the talking.

Every cut is dry-aged on-site, finished over oak and olive wood, and brought to the table the way it left the fire — unhurried, and a little smoking.
